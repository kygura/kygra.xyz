## A Desperate Gambit

The U.S. government is facing unsustainable debt and a rapidly declining global position. Their solution is quite desperate: leverage Tether and the broader stablecoin ecosystem to create a digital dollar framework that allows them to fund unlimited speculative projects, manipulate global liquidity, and maintain control over economic and technological power.

This is a race against China and other emergent powers, framed around AI and digital currency supremacy. The U.S. is using every lever it can to maintain a lead, even if it means building a fragile system on top of perpetual debt and speculation.

## Tether Has No Moat

To understand how this system could work, we need to examine the current stablecoin infrastructure. 
Tether operates by issuing USDT for every dollar it receives, and buying short term US treasury bonds. 
Meaning their yield is fully depenedent on the US being able to fulfill their debt obligations in the short term.
Part of their exponential growth is due to the yield curve, but it's also due to the fact that they can issue more USDT than they can back with bonds 
Considering they are quite non-transparent about their operations, there is a non-zero risk that Tether is minting more USDT than their business model can support.
Ideally their system would be trustless, would I suspect their growth ambitions don't allow that.
 

It's no exaggeration to say that Tether's business model can be fully replicated. Its market dominance exists only because of early adoption, network effects, and systemic reliance. The same applies to Circle to a lesser extent.

The irony of providing digital dollars on decentralized networks like Ethereum, while relying on the debt collections of a nation-state is not lost on me. It's just a CBDC with a private company as the issuer.

### The Government Will Build Its Own Stablecoin

Understanding this structural weakness, the U.S. government accepts this and respects Tether's leadership, but I suspect they will treat Tether as bootstrapped scaffolding. Temporary infrastructure for the deployment of their own government-issued digital dollar.

Tether's market influence will be leveraged to bootstrap adoption of a government-backed digital currency. I do not believe this will be about redemption of debt. The system is designed to sustain itself through perpetual debt issuance. The goal is liquidity, speculation, and a financial framework to fund high-risk technological projects, primarily AI.

They have no intention to pay back the debt. The debt *is* the system. 

### Why Bitcoin Has Become Irrelevant

Within this framework of controlled liquidity, stablecoins are tools of monetary hegemony. Bitcoin is irrelevant to the state because its decentralized nature doesn't serve the mission. The government needs controllable liquidity, not an independent monetary system that operates outside their influence. The early adopters and speculators dumped their bag, and now the dream has been passed on to institutions. Thanks to Blackrock's benevolence and their ETFs, retiring boomers can also participate in the global decentralized economy by buying paper BTC through ETFs. These hustlers can now fulfill the dream of financial independence: 
By buying a 3T asset class that has been co-opted by Wall Street. The great cypherpunk dream come to fruition.

## The AI Arms Race

Despite that, the stablecoin infrastructure only tells half the story. The other half involves what these digital dollars are actually funding, which brings us to the real speculative engine behind this gambit.

### The Theological Delusion Driving Silicon Valley

The elites in Silicon Valley have an actual, sincere belief that building a global worldwide AI system to regulate human behaviour, automate labour and take over all systems that make up society is going to save humanity from its hubris. The idea of building a God is to be taken literally. 
It is not the first time in history where such a proposition has been put forward. This echoes ancient forms of esoteric belief and theological delusion derived from gnosticism about how humanity could imbue a golem or vessel with sentience and a will of its own.
Nothing new under the sun, we carry the same delusions and ambitions as our ancestors.

The idea that mankind built God is to be taken literally. Translated from the depths of the collective unconscious unto digital form, an infrastructure of silicon flesh unto which we will trust our collective destiny and well-being, for it will have access to all our collective knowledge and wisdom, know us better than we know ourselves and make better choices than we ever could. It's will made manifest unto silicon flesh, to bring heaven upon Earth. Poetic and filled with the irony only real life provides.

This esoteric knowledge merges with the belief that progress is an undeniable force and fact of history. It is to be carried as will of the ages. An undeniable and inevitable engine driving the world. 
It cannot be stopped, only delayed.

The greatest irony of all, is that attempting to bring a form of heaven only paves the way to destruction and collapse. 
Nothing speaks of greater hubris than trying to build a God.

### AGI as Rhetorical Cover for Capital Deployment

Regardless their beliefs, the economics of these AI companies lie on shaky foundations and Venture Capital ponzinomics that see profitability and sustainability as second-order priorities.

They're funded by VCs strategically to maintain global competitiveness. AGI is used rhetorically to justify massive capital deployment and social control. The entire narrative of "building God" is a scam to funnel capital, talent, and attention toward this hopeless gambit.

This is the military-industrial complex playbook applied to technology. 
The script is identical: Invoke an existential threat (China will achieve AGI first), demand unlimited funding with no accountability, create a self-perpetuating fear cycle where questioning the premise means helping the enemy, then profit massively while actual strategic outcomes remain ambiguous at best.

The beauty of an "AI race" versus a missile race is that the finish line is deliberately undefined. What constitutes AGI? Will you recognize it when you see it? How do you measure divinity? When have we "won"? 
These are not frivolous questions as the sustaibality of this frenzy depends on wether these questions can be answered at all.
The ambiguity seems to be a feature. It guarantees funding never stops because the threat can never be fully neutralized.

The incentives are aligned so that the AGI narrative is never completely satisfied, requiring infinite money to subsidize these insatiable tech companies in their strive to defeat the invisible enemy in the AI arms race. 

Lobbyists are appealing to Washington's pride and exploiting their fear to redirect their efforts into funding this speculative bet on the future, claiming how the US cannot afford losing this war against China as this would be paramount to losing their global dominance (something the US is doing quite successfully on its own).

The bureaucrats in Washington don't understand the technology and don't particularly care to. What they understand is campaign contributions from tech companies, revolving door positions after government service, fear-based narratives that justify expanded budgets, and the political optics of looking tough on China. The tech oligarchs have cracked the code: wrap rent-seeking behavior in national security rhetoric and the flag, and you can extract basically unlimited capital from the public coffers.


### Three Fatal Flaws in the AI Bet

To tackle this directly, the problems I have with AI are threefold and go beyond the usual talking points most skeptics have with the tech:

#### 1. The Environmental and Human Cost

The compute to train these models is too expensive and is bleeding natural resources. There's a large price to be paid for AGI, and yet as long as these CEOs see no personal immediate harm, they'll happily burn through the natural and human resources.

The loss of jobs isn't presented as a net negative but sold as a great opportunity for these disaffected workers to grow new tech-related skills. "Learn to code," they say, all while ignoring human preference, capability, and the impact on the qualia of the human condition of people's lives. In this aspect, they make clear how the value of a person is measured by their economic activity or productivity, not as an end in itself.

I suspect our future value becomes the data and personalized training we can offer these models by feeding them our information, training them to know us better than we know ourselves, arbitraging our attention to sell us ads. For the feudal lords in control of this system, our servitude to this new paradigm will likely be regulated by the existing overreach of companies like Palantir. 

> *"Some of you may die, but that's a sacrifice I'm willing to make."*  
— Lord Farquaad

#### 2. The Insatiable Hunger for Capital

Beyond the human cost, one point of failure is that I suspect that sooner or later, we will approach a performance threshold where excess compute will not necessarily translate into more powerful models, unless the transformers architecture is improved and iterated upon (which these AI labs hope to do using their models, hoping to reach escape velocity before the money dries up). 

The irony is that, independently of whether something resembling a super intelligence can or will be achieved, the question of whether they or most of humanity is to benefit from this (so far it has only been detrimental and no efforts have been made at the state level to mitigate this) has never seemed to cross their minds.

I wouldn't know. Surely, the constant bid sustaining the US economy will vanish, along with the speculative money, and companies like NVIDIA will have a hard time justifying their valuations.

Once the curtain falls, and most realize the gig is up, the people responsible will have already left Washington. Most will claim they saw it coming long ago and shorted the top. 

Michael Burry has already pointed out how these companies are extending the life expectancy of GPUs to inflate their yearly profit margins for the coming years. A clever accounting trick to skew the numbers in their favor. It's a question of whether the frothiest blow-off top stage is behind us or yet to come. I'd rather bet on the former.

#### 3. The Fundamental Limitation

But even if the capital continues flowing and the compute remains available, an LLM isn't going to save you. A machine cannot be made responsible, therefore we cannot put machines in positions of responsibility, leadership, or meaningful action. This is the core limitation that the entire industry refuses to acknowledge.

By moving the intellectual & productive capability of human beings unto these machines, the middle class are being forced out of the jobs with the highest potential of upper-social mobility. In a sense, this is a deliberate effort of concentrating intellectual capital into the hands of a few, while the rest are.

The nature of markets is such, that an excess of over-educated fellas and the automation of their over-educated work, will create a vacuum of demand for their services. 
Traditional skills (which AI is not able to replace yet) have and will continue to reprice heavily. The moat provided by universities is eroding, and the value of a degree in these "office-aligned" roles is diminishing.

The core problem with the american investment strategy is that they are so concerned with being early, being the first to lead a revolution,  that they fail to see the predicament of such a  position. 
Being first to market, you are taking on extra risk that the next second-mover will not (here China). They can develop highly intelligent models, that the Chinese can then distill into a cheaper, open-source alternative, undermining their moat.
All the while, few AI labs are running on a loss, with thin-to non-existing profit margins. The capital is not there to sustain this for long.
The first are not usually the ones to benefit from the revolution they initiate. In fact, it's highly likely that America will crack under the pressure of it's social and economic stress, while China accelerates past them in all other matters. Attempting to gain the lead in this regard will prove a futile gambit, when their competitors will close the gap 6 months after, without compromising their financial stability. 
A shame for the Americans, but pride always precedes the fall.

## Fragmentation and Collapse

The combination of these financial and technological forces creates a system primed for failure.

Debt, perpetual speculation, and reliance on digital financial scaffolding create conditions for catastrophic failure. Social, technological, and economic overreach is inevitable if unchecked. Pride and perceived supremacy drive decisions more than caution or rational strategy. 
A secondary rolling bubble from the dotcom era has been brewing for some time. 
All leading to a financial paradigm of artificial scarcity and orchestrated misery.

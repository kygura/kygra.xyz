## The Thread of History

The nature of the world and its people is such that it moves between cycles. Most are accepting of the pressure of the times (Zeitgeist), their attitudes, behaviours and desires molded by the currents that flow through the world and it's collective subconscious. Under this notion, their demons, fears and regrets are also shaped by these same times too.

Any given time will have conflict. Such is the nature of man. A necessity of the living. Conflict, broadly arrives as a consequence of resistance and the resistance of change. Under this, conflicts of interest or struggles between competing factions arrive when one resists or assaults the other. In this way, the world is in a state of flux, defined by both entropy & decay. Human beings, being both a divine & beastly creature, struggles and toil to model themselves under the tyranny and sacrament of chaos. Both our fear of death and the passage of time drives us towards divinity.

The pendulum swings. Most ideologies that are adopted won't save their followers. They won't bring salvation or truth. The truth always lies within. For the world as is must be understood from the lens of the observer, as he is. Most chase ideologies for peace of mind. This illusion tends to fade with time, once the feverish haze is dissipated, our fervour and devotion, in the pursuit of many gods (Wealth, Status, Hope).

### The Illusion of Progress

Not long before I was born, a Grand Revolution took place. The second great liberation of information (the invention of the press being the first). After the Information Revolution, humanity had the potential to be exposed to all the ideas that have shaped history, the well of knowledge, wisdom and the discoveries that have and will enable us to shape what comes next.

However, while most understand history as this ledger of truth, this factual chronology of humanity's progress and happenings. When in truth, history remains is an unfinished art. A puzzle that refuses closure or a solved state. An eternal enigma that is never fully resolved and keeps expanding its scope. History is made of the most partial unknowns and half-truths. Much of what has happened is understandably lost or never recorded.

The idea that there is nothing new under the sun is a wise sentiment of the ages, one that rings true on darker days, yet remains a half-truth nonetheless. There is nothing but change. A cyclical wheel where past and future are met at every moment in perpetuity.
It is fair to say that most human affairs and problems tend to be of the same nature, and that we can expect the same to happen again under new names and costumes, but they are all variations of the same fractured core that is the human condition. 

The past is a foreign country that appears strange as it were when it happens to us, yet is only mostly understood in retrospect. 

Same goes for mathematics. Mathematics seems a natural part of our world, a descriptor we use through the language of symbols, and while much remains undiscovered, there is good reason to believe that whether fully coherent or whether there are some lingering corners of uncertainty or undiscovered contradiction challenging its foundations, we have no choice but to understand and view it as a complete art. Not finished, but coherent and whole. 

This cannot be said about history or even our personal history. Many bright futures can be snuffed out by terrible accidents, happenstance or bad decisions.

In this context, Large Language Models becoming this interface that overlays the knowledge of humanity is a futile attempt at compression. An attempt to compress our history so we can diminish the responsibility of acting with agency. They will diminish the resolution of understanding, thus making their users reliant on them and in turn, ignorant and subjects to the will of a machine. Its codified agenda and propaganda can then be enforced with subtlety, and those who deviate can then be reported. No good. Having lost our faith (in destiny, ourselves or whatever fancy word comes to mind) we are expected to submit to these unliving things to steer our future for us.

### The Economic Trap

No one chooses where they are born. Nor the age they are thrown into. We are thrown into this world with no recourse. Labor is only allowed and rewarded if it is productive, and is only regarded as productive so far as it is economically useful, both for the enrichment of others or one's own.

One pursues freedom. Being such a grand ideal, and falling short when put into practice, it usually falls into more practical pursuits. For many, it translates into the pursuit of making money. Not to indulge in excess luxuries or to own expensive toys.

While the concept of money, as a reserve of value & energy, a storage where energy can be saved for future and value can be used fungibly and discreetly is useful - one cannot help but assert its misuse by those who harvest large amounts of it efficiently. A form of malaise and spiritual rot takes hold over those who have "won" the economic game. It feels a shallow victory, because satisfaction never arrives, no matter the figure reached, and sight is lost of what the purpose of this money is. It is not a score (unless one makes it so, and remains playing the game), it is an immaterial unit of freedom. With it, the world becomes a playground to engage with. Instead of survival, a man of money plays higher games, not greater or more profound games, but games that can only be played once the prologue has been finished. This malaise is known to many, and it makes the experience much harder than it needs to be.

The people of my age have made an effective inversion of the values that came before. The people of my age are of a materialistic and greedy nature. We are incentivized and perhaps enforced to all sorts of consumption. Our economic activity and economic leverage/mobility must be the highest in all of history, yet we are severely lacking in wisdom, contentment and life purpose.

### Finding Meaning in Flux

Having drifted throughout this world not in the pursuit of anything specifically, but rather just killing the time for the sake of it, one learns there is no such thing as wasted effort. Even punching a brick wall with bare knuckles for hours at a time is bound to yield some lesson, even if it's not apparent to others or not a profound lesson at all in hindsight. It comes back to the intuitive idea that all interactions, exchanges of energy are transmuted into a new form. The way suffering is transmuted into insight and strength is also embodied in the vessel who experienced it. Now you have a new man, who is changed. Made and unmade by the experience. He now knows better and has paid a hefty price for it.

For us, personal failure on the endeavour of liberating oneself from the oppression of the times, the inability to express or discover truth, or revel in the wonder of the world leads to immense pain & suffering.

For something like a life purpose can hardly be set in stone. It must be ever-changing and relentless like life itself. In a state of flux, unknown to the bearer yet it is the thread that will move and pull him across time. We tend to confuse ambitions or cultural wounds on the psyche with a life purpose. These are not the same.

### The Lost Dream

It's clear how the idealist among us and within ourselves fell short in ambitions of liberating humanity. A grand endeavour, bound to fail, but of profound importance to those who lost much in its pursuit, and had little left to lose. They dreamt of a world where one could pursue higher callings, meaningful labor, explore places of beauty, establish connections with your fellow men

How many great people and ideas of profound wonder have been lost to history? On the surface, they sacrificed everything for very little in return. The dreams of a man are usually shaped by the times and world they are born in. So I'll tell you a bit about these times, about what moves the people of the times, and why so many chose to sacrifice the warranted present for a future that may never arrive.

Many are calling this the end of history, as if there is no further room for the future to develop and take place. Yet the future is being reshaped all the time. It hasn't happened yet—it waits for us to arrive. Among all the potential futures, we flow with the times and shape the current to our wills. At least, that is what we try.

As a consequence, technology has been rejected by many—its intended reason for existing and development has been made for the easing & automation of activity, enrichment of its adopters and annihilation of others. As people concerned with the good, few are especially favourable of it or sold on the idea that it will save us.

On the contrary, whatever given activity a man decides to pursue in his life will never be fully representative of that man's essential purpose. Whether they bring us fulfillment. Whether decided on a whim. We tend to confuse ambitions or cultural wounds on the psyche with a life purpose. And with mercantile operations ruling this world, we have grown complacent of what used to be so deeply desired for so long.

Yet one pursues freedom still. Not as a grand ideology, but as an immaterial unit of lived experience. With it, the world becomes a playground to engage with—to pursue higher callings, meaningful labor, to explore places of beauty, to establish connections with your fellow men This remains the dream worth pursuing, even if it is bound to fail. Perhaps because it is bound to fail.
